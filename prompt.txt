Please add a new column called player_category and assign each player A, B, or C.

---

CLASSIFICATION PHILOSOPHY

Do not apply a single scoring formula to every player. A bowler should never be judged against a batter's run tally, and a batter should not be penalised for having zero wickets. Each player must be evaluated in the context of what their role demands.

The goal is: A = genuinely elite at their role, B = solid and reliable, C = limited IPL output or developing player.

---

STEP 1 — Identify true role

Use the role column first. Then cross-check with stats to confirm or override:
- If role contains "Batter" / "Batsman" / "WK" → BATTER
- If role contains "Bowler" → BOWLER
- If role contains "Allrounder" → ALLROUNDER
- If role is blank or unclear: check stats
  - bowl_wickets > 20 AND bat_runs > 200 → ALLROUNDER
  - bowl_wickets > 20 → BOWLER
  - bat_runs > 200 → BATTER
  - Otherwise → BATTER (default)

---

STEP 2 — Score each player within their role group

Treat blank stats as 0. Ignore stats from players with fewer than 5 bat_matches (mark them C automatically — insufficient sample size).

BATTER scoring — compare only against other BATTERS:
  Key stats: bat_runs, bat_average, bat_sr, bat_50s, bat_100s
  Weights:   35%       25%          20%     10%      10%
  Normalize each stat 0–1 within the BATTER group (divide by that group's max).
  bat_score = weighted sum × 100

BOWLER scoring — compare only against other BOWLERS:
  Key stats: bowl_wickets, bowl_eco (lower=better), bowl_avg (lower=better), bowl_5w, bowl_4w
  Weights:   40%            20%                       20%                       10%      10%
  Normalize bowl_wickets, bowl_5w, bowl_4w: higher = better
  Normalize bowl_eco, bowl_avg: invert → (max − value) / max within the BOWLER group
  If bowl_wickets = 0: bowl_score = 0
  bowl_score = weighted sum × 100

ALLROUNDER scoring — compare only against other ALLROUNDERS:
  bat_score  = same formula as BATTER group but normalised within ALLROUNDER group
  bowl_score = same formula as BOWLER group but normalised within ALLROUNDER group
  Determine dominant skill:
    - If bat_runs > 800 AND bowl_wickets < 20  → batting-dominant: final = bat_score × 0.65 + bowl_score × 0.35
    - If bowl_wickets > 30 AND bat_runs < 400  → bowling-dominant: final = bat_score × 0.35 + bowl_score × 0.65
    - Otherwise (true all-rounder)              → final = bat_score × 0.50 + bowl_score × 0.50 + 5 versatility bonus

---

STEP 3 — Assign A / B / C within each role group

For each role group (BATTER, BOWLER, ALLROUNDER) independently:
  - Sort players by their final score descending
  - Top third    → A
  - Middle third → B
  - Bottom third → C
  - Players auto-assigned C in Step 2 (< 5 matches) stay C

This ensures an A-rated bowler is elite among bowlers, not just better than batters who never bowl.

STEP 4 — Output

Return the full CSV with player_category added as the last column.
